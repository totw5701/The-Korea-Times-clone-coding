Will COVID-19 pandemic die down with vaccine rollout?

New virus cases fall below 900

Korea's daily new coronavirus cases fell below 900 on Saturday largely due to less testing over the New Year's Day holiday, as health authorities are expected to extend the current social distancing rules. The country added 824 more COVID-19 cases, including 788 local infections, raising the total caseload to 62,593, according to the Korea Disease Control and Prevention Agency (KDCA). It reported 25 additional deaths from COVID-19, raising the death toll to 942. The latest infection count marked a decrease from 1,029 cases Friday. The country logged 807 cases Monday, 1,045 cases Tuesday, 1,050 cases Wednesday and 967 cases Thursday. Over the past seven days, the daily tallies have hovered around 1,000.
